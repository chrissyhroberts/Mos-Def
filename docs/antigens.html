7.1 Protocol

⌚ Timing: 2-3 hours, but can be split into shorter periods if needed (see Tip 2).

This protocol takes place at ambient temperature. All incubations are at ambient temperature.

Tip: Keep the proteins as cold as possible, for as long as possible, to avoid degradation. Work with tubes on ice or in chilled racks. Likewise, thaw proteins that are in solution on ice.

Tip 2: If time is limited, prepare a smaller number of proteins at one time, completing the full process of rehydration, aliquotting and storage for these, and return another time to complete the next batch.

  1. Prepare filter-sterlised PBS as per Buffers and Reagents protocol.

  2. Label protein lo-bind tubes with antigen name, catalogue number, concentration, volume and expiry date. The approximate number of tubes needed for each antigen is shown in column 6 in Table 1 below.

  3. Rehydrate lyophylised antigens using the volumes of PBS listed in column 2 of Table 1.

    NOTE: MxA comes in solution and does not need rehydrating. See manufacturer’s data sheet for concentration.

  4. Leave vials to sit for 60 seconds to rehydrate, then vortex gently, spin down.

  5. Aliquot into the volumes and number of tubes indicated in columns 5 and 6 of Table 1.

  6. Store aliquots at -70 or below immediately.
