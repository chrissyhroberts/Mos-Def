5  Bead Coupling

We provide two versions of this protocol for coupling enough beads for 8 plates using 120 µL of bead stock (Option A), or a larger-scale coupling which provides enough beads for about 52 plates using 500 µL of bead stock (Option B).

Variations on the number of beads may be calculated using the Luminex Cookbook scaling table.

5.3 Preparation

This protocol uses EDC [1-ethyl-3-(3-dimethylaminopropyl)carbodiimide hydrochloride)]. EDC absorbs moisture from the air. It is helpful to aliquot it into 50 mg, single-use, amounts in screw cap tubes on receipt and to store these in individual ziplock bags with desiccant, at -20° C.

For quality assurance processes, we recommend keeping note of the lot numbers and expiry dates of both beads and capture antibodies. The diagnostic performance of beads changes with time and we do not recommend using coupled beads that are more than two months old.

5.4 Protocol A - Coupling beads for 8 plates

⌚ Timing: 2 hours, then 2 hours incubation, 1h45m to finish. Total approx. 5h45m.

This protocol takes place at ambient temperature. All incubations are at ambient temperature.

Tip: Protect beads from light with a foil cover when they are not being manipulated such as during incubations.

  1. Label tubes (Eppendorf, protein lo-bind) one for each coupling, with at least antigen name and bead region.

  2. Vortex and sonicate the bead stocks for ~60 seconds of each. Vortex some more immediately before pipetting.

  3. Transfer 120 µL of bead stock (1.5 x 106 beads for 8 full plates) into each respective tube.

  4. Put tubes in magnet rack for 60 s.

  5. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  6. Remove tubes from magnet rack. Add 100 µL dH2O. Vortex & sonicate for ~20 s of each.

  7. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  8. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  9. Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 80 µL Activation buffer (0.1 M sodium phosphate, pH 6.2). Vortex & sonicate, ~20 s each.

  10. Make up 50 mg/mL sulfo-NHS (Thermo, 24510) and 50 mg/mL EDC (Thermo, 22980) in Activation buffer, in separate tubes. Use max 25 mg in 500 µL (for 12 couplings).

  11. Add 10 µL of the sulfo-NHS solution to the beads. Vortex.

  12. Add 10 µL of the EDC solution to the beads. Vortex.

  13. Incubate 10 mins @ room temp. Vortex. Incubate for further 10 mins.

  14. During the incubation, set up new tubes for Ab dilution in MES buffer.

  15. To 400 µL MES, minus the volume of Ab, add in this much of each Ab:

Antibody on bead Ab catalogue number (bio-techne unless stated) Antibody amount per million beads Volume of capture antibody needed for 1.5e6 beads (µL)
Ang-1 MAB9231 5 7.5
Ang-2 NB110-85467 5 7.5
Azu NBP2-12045 7 10.5
CHI3L1 MAB25991 4.5 6.8
IL-6 MAB206 5 7.5
IL-8 M801 (Thermo) 5 7.5
IL-10 MAB2172 4 6.0
IP-10 MAB266 5 7.5
MxA MA5-24914 (Thermo) 6 9.0
sTNF-R1 MAB225 6 9.0
sTREM1 H00054210-M04 6 9.0
TRAIL MAB375 6 9.0

  1. Set these Ab tubes aside and continue with the beads.

  2. Bead tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  3. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  4. MES wash 1: Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 250 µL MES buffer. Vortex & sonicate for ~ 20 s each.

  5. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  6. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  7. MES wash 2: Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 250 µL MES buffer. Vortex & sonicate for ~ 20 s each.

  8. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  9. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  10. Add 100 µL MES buffer to each bead tube.

  11. Transfer 400 µL of each antibody dilution into the respective bead tubes and vortex.

  12. Incubate 2 hours on mixer at room temp. Start time _______________

  13. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  14. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  15. Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 500 µL PBS-TBN. Vortex & sonicate for ~20 s.

  16. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  17. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  18. PBS-TBN wash 1: Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 1 mL PBS-TBN. Vortex & sonicate for ~20 s.

  19. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  20. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  21. PBS-TBN wash 2: Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 1 mL PBS-TBN. Vortex & sonicate for ~20 s.

  22. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  23. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  24. Remove tube from magnet rack. Resuspend coupled beads in 200 µL PBS-TBN. Vortex.

  25. Ensure newly coupled beads are fully labelled.

  26. Store the coupled beads in the fridge.

5.5 Protocol B - Coupling beads for 52 plates

⌚ Timing: 2 hours, then 2 hours incubation, 1h45m to finish. Total approx. 5h45m.

The protocol takes place at ambient temperature. All incubations are at ambient temperature.

  1. Label tubes (Eppendorf, protein lo-bind) one for each coupling.

  2. Vortex and sonicate the bead stocks for ~60 s of each. Vortex some more immediately before pipetting.

  3. Transfer 0.5 mL of bead stock (6.25 x 106 beads for ~52 full plates) into each respective tube.

  4. Put tube in magnet rack for 60 s.

  5. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  6. Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 100 µL dH2O. Vortex & sonicate for ~20 s of each.

  7. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  8. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  9. Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 80 µL Activation buffer. Vortex & sonicate, ~20 s each.

  10. In separate tubes, make up 50 mg/mL sulfo-NHS and 50 mg/mL EDC in Activation buffer. These should always be made fresh, immediately before use, and not stored.

  11. Add 10 µL of the sulfo-NHS solution to the beads. Vortex.

  12. Add 10 µL of the EDC solution to the beads. Vortex.

  13. Incubate 10 mins @ room temp. Vortex. Incubate for further 10 mins.

  14. During the incubation, set up 1.5 mL tubes for antibody dilution in MES buffer. One tube per bead region.

  15. Dispense 400 µL MES to each tube.

  16. Remove the MES volume indicated for each Ab in the table below (bold numbers).

  17. Add in the indicated amount of Ab stock:

  1. Bead tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  2. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  3. MES wash 1: Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 500 µL MES buffer. Vortex & sonicate for ~ 20 s each.

  4. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  5. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  6. MES wash 2: Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 500 µL MES buffer. Vortex & sonicate for ~ 20 s each.

  8. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  9. Add 100 µL MES buffer to beads.

  10. Transfer all 400 µL of each antibody dilution into the respective bead tubes and vortex. (So the antibody amount is eventually diluted in 0.5 mL of MES by the time it is incubated with the beads, as per scale-up table).

  11. Incubate 2 hours on mixer at room temp. Start time _______________

  12. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  13. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  14. Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 0.5 mL PBS-TBN. Vortex & sonicate for ~20 s.

  15. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  16. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  17. PBS-TBN wash 1: Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 1 mL PBS-TBN. Vortex & sonicate for ~20 s.

  18. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  19. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  20. PBS-TBN wash 2: Remove tube from magnet rack. Add 1 mL PBS-TBN. Vortex & sonicate for ~20 s.

  21. Tube into magnet rack, 60 s.

  22. Remove supernatant without disturbing beads.

  23. Remove tube from magnet rack. Resuspend coupled beads in 830 µL PBS-TBN. Vortex. This is 1.66x the original volume (500 µL) to give a theoretical count of 7.5 x 106 beads/mL.

  24. Fully label and store the newly coupled beads in the fridge.
