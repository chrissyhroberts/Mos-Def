4  Buffers and Reagents

4.1 Buffers for general assay procedures

4.1.1 Deionised water (dH2O)

Filter 50 mL of deionised water through a 0.2 µm filter into a sterile container.

4.1.2 PBS

1 tablet (Sigma; P4417) per 200 mL deionised water.

4.1.3 PBST (wash buffer)

PBS + 0.5 mL/L (0.05% v/v) Tween 20

Make fresh every week.

4.1.4 PBS-TBN (blocking/storage/assay buffer)

PBST + 10 mg/mL BSA + 0.5 mg/mL sodium azide

Per 100 mL PBST, add:

  • 1 g BSA
  • 500 µL of 10% (100 mg/mL) sodium azide solution in deionised water
    • *CAUTION* - use appropriate PPE and safety measures when handling solid and liquids with sodium azide.

Filter through 0.2 µm filter and store in a fridge until use.

4.2 Buffers for bead coupling

4.2.1 Activation buffer

  • 0.1 M sodium phosphate monobasic, pH 6.2
  • 1.19 g NaH2PO4 (Sigma, S8282)
  • 100 mL dH2O

Adjust pH to 6.2 with 5 or 10 M NaOH Filter through 0.2 µm filter and store in a fridge until use.

4.2.2 Blocking / Storage / Assay buffer PBS-TBN

  • PBST + 10 mg/mL BSA + 0.5 mg/mL sodium azide
  • 500 µL of 10% (100 mg/mL) sodium azide solution in water.
    • *CAUTION* - use appropriate PPE and safety measures.
  • Per 100 mL PBST = 1 g BSA
  • 50 µL Tween (if made in PBS)

4.2.3 Coupling buffer

  • 50 mM MES, pH 5.0
    • 0.976 g MES (Sigma, M3671)
    • 100 mL dH2O

Adjust pH to 5 with 5 or 10 M NaOH Filter through 0.2 µm filter and store in a fridge until use.

4.2.4 PBS

  • 1 tablet (Sigma; P4417) per 200 mL dH2O

4.2.5 Wash buffer PBST

  • PBS + 0.5 mL/L Tween 20
